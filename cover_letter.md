# Cover Letter

Dear Editor,

I am submitting our manuscript "Evaluating Citation Reliability and Verifiability in LLM-Assisted Academic Writing" for consideration for publication.

## Summary

This paper presents a controlled empirical evaluation of four common LLM-assisted academic writing workflows, comparing their citation reliability. We generated 120 related-work paragraphs using DeepSeek-chat across 30 research questions, extracted 950 references, and verified each against Crossref, OpenAlex, and arXiv APIs.

## Key Contributions

1. The first systematic comparison of four writing workflows (direct, cautious prompting, retrieval-first, verify-and-repair) for citation reliability.
2. Evidence that only 59.1% of AI-generated references can be verified as existing works.
3. Demonstration that simulated retrieval-first writing performs worse than direct generation.
4. Evidence that Chinese-language prompts produce lower citation verifiability.
5. A reproduceable experimental pipeline for citation verification.

## Relevance

This work is directly relevant to researchers using AI for academic writing, tool developers building AI-assisted writing systems, and journal editors concerned about citation integrity. The findings have implications for academic integrity policies and the design of verification tools.
